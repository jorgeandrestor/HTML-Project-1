Top 5 Sneakers In My Collection

Number 5- Adidas Alphaboost in Grey

Releasing in April 2019, the Adidas Alphaboost was a combination of two of Adida's most popular sillhouettes- the Alphabounce and the UltraBoost.
The sneaker is considered to be "GR" (general release), and is easily found in stores and online for MSRP (manufacturer's suggested retail price ) or lower.

Fit

Adidas's Boost technology makes the shoes incredibly comfortable underfoot for days spent standing in front of a classroom, and the breathable material makes it a very comfortable summer shoe.

Looks- 7/10
Comfort- 10/10
Obtainability/Cost- 10/10

Number 4- Adidas Yeezy 350 V2 Black

The first colorway of the Yeezy 350 V2 released in September of 2016, a year after Kanye West saw great success with his Yeezy 350 V1. The black colorway pictured here released in November of 2019, and many other colorways are constantly releasing. Unfortunately, due to the popularity of the brand, these sneakers are hard to come by without turning to reseller markets, but several re-releases have improved the availability pool, overall lowering prices.

Fit

In terms of fit, the low cut and Boost technology make these very comfortable in terms of the sole of the foot. The breathable material makes this another ideal summer shoe, but it should generally be avoided on cold winter days or any time there is rain out. Unfortunately, the narrowness of the toebox restricts comfort for wearers with wide feet.

Looks- 9.5/10
Comfort- 7/10
Obtainability/Cost- 5/10

Number 3- Air Jordan 1 Shadow and Air Jordan 1 Royal Blue

The iconic Air Jordan 1 first released in 1995, and was Michael Jordan's first signature shoe. Although the most iconic colorway of the original releases is the black and red (nicknamed "Bred") colroway, the black and gray "Shadow" colorway pictured is lauded for its flexibility in fitting into many different outfits. The Shadow 1s have been re-released ( "retroed") 4 times, with the most recent being in 2018. However, the high demand for the colorway coupled with a relatively limited release has some sizes selling at 4 or 5 times the MSRP.

Similarly, the Royal Blue colorway (the favorite colorway of this website's author) was also an original (OG) colorway release, and has retroed several times, most recently in 2017. Again, due to limited quantity and popular demand, this is another sneaker that sells for many times its initial value on reseller markets.

Fit

This is the first high-top shoe on the list, which comes with its pros and cons. A high top offers a lot of ankle support, but takes more time to put on. On a more subjective note, high top sneakers are generally considered to not be summer sneakers, with many opting for low tops instead. A slightly narrow toebox and limited cushioning underfoot makes these slightly less comfortable to wear throughout an entire day.

Looks- 9/10
Comfort- 6/10
Obtainability/Cost- 3/10

Number 2- Vans Sk8-HI in Navy

The SK8-HI is a sneaker that saw a rise in popularity in the 1970s, and has been a staple of many people's wardrobes since. The model first found success amongst skaters, who liked the sneaker for its affordability and looks. Today, the SK8-HI has transcended many different cultural circles, and is a really excellently priced and cool looking option for those looking to get into the sneaker game. The SK8-HI is easily found on many websites and in stores, and the Navy colorway pictures is both very popular and very easy to purchase.

Fit

Another high top that makes the list, these sneakers are, in the writer's opinion, a lot more flexible than the Air Jordan 1s in terms of style. The canvas upper makes them relatively breathable, enabling them for more adequate use as a summer shoe. There is limited cushioning underfoot, but the rubber outsole makes them an excellent dancing shoe, and a larger toebox allows for more comfort for wide-foot wearers.

Looks- 8.5/10
Comfort- 8/10
Obtainability/Cost- 10/10

Number 1- Nike Air Force 1 Low in White

The Air Force 1 High Top was Nike's first entry into the basketball sneaker game. A low version released in 1982, the same year as the high top. Although the Air Force 1s were discontinued in the 80's, they have returned full force in the 21st century, becoming one of the most iconic sneakers in the world. With countless mentions in musical lyrics, wide use across pivotal sneaker markets (basketball players, skaters, dancers), and a silhouette that is considered timeless, the Air Force 1 may be the most popular shoe on this list. Affordability is also a huge factor here- at $90.00 USD, the cost of entry for a pair of these sneakers is very low compared to others on this list. Plus, white goes with everything!

Fit

The lowtop version of this shoe is very easy to slip on and off. The Air Max technology used in the sole of the sneaker makes it incredibly comfortable to wear for long days, and the ample toebox allows a lot of comfort for sneakerheads with wide feet. The breathability allows the AF1s to be used in the summer, but the solid construction makes it a passable winter shoes as well.

Looks- 10/10
Comfort- 9/10
Obtainability/Cost- 10/10
